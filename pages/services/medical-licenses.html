Healthcare Licensing FAQs

Common questions about medical licenses and registrations

KPME (Karnataka Private Medical Establishments) registration is mandatory for all private healthcare facilities in Karnataka including clinics, nursing homes, hospitals, diagnostic centers, and pathology labs. It ensures compliance with healthcare standards and regulations.

KPME registration typically takes 30-45 days from document submission to license issuance. This includes application processing, site inspection, and final approval by the district health authority.

Key documents include: Doctor's qualification certificates, KMC registration, premises ownership/rental documents, fire safety certificate, biomedical waste agreement, property tax receipt, and identity proofs of the doctor/owner.

Yes, biomedical waste management authorization is mandatory for all healthcare facilities generating biomedical waste, regardless of size. It's regulated by the Pollution Control Board and requires proper segregation, storage, and disposal.
